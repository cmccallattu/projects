Recipe #1 - Etouffee

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 10 average people, 20 children, or 5 big dudes
Ingredients Quantity
Onion 1 large
Crushed Tomatoes 1 large can
Celery 1 bundle
Celery 1 bundle

Step 1: Begin by dicing the large onion, mincing all of the garlic, dicing the carrot, cutting the celery,
and cutting the chicken breast into bite-sized pieces

Step 2: Once you have prepared these ingredients, you may begin to prepare your brown roux. Start
melting the stick and a half of butter in a large pan. Once this has fully melted, add your cup and a half
of flour.You will need to begin stirring this flour in immediately to help break up clumps. Your roux
should be on medium heat at this point. Keep an eye on your roux and stir periodically to keep from
burning.

Step 3: Once the roux is going, you will need to coat the bottom of your large stock pot with olive oil
and begin cooking your onions on medium high heat after the oil has heated up. After a few minutes
of cooking the onions you will add the diced carrot and celery. Stir briskly and allow these vegetables
to sweat for a few minutes before adding the garlic. Allow the garlic to sweat for an additional few
minutes before you start adding the chicken stock. Go ahead and add all of the chicken stock and turn
up the heat to high. Once this mixture begins to boil, add the beer and allow that to cook off for a few
minutes. Allow the mixture to begin boiling again before adding the chicken, seasoning and hot sauce.

At this point you can add the can of crushed tomato to the stock pot and bring everything to a high
simmer.
